The AI Supply Chain Hack That Turned Security Into the Problem

This hack just surfaced recently, and it’s honestly wild. There’s a Python library called LiteLLM used by giants like Stripe, Netflix, and Google. If you’re building anything with AI in Python, there’s a good chance it’s somewhere in your stack.

"Reports suggest the attackers may have compromised Trivy first, which is a security scanning tool used in LiteLLM’s build pipeline."

A security tool became the attack vector. When the scanner ran, it exposed publishing credentials, letting attackers push malicious updates that appeared legitimate.

The takeaway? Pin your dependencies. Use lockfiles. And maybe don’t assume your security tools are always secure.
